Computer Science Engineering

Bachelor of Engineering in Computer Science (BE CSE) is a 4-year undergraduate engineering program that focuses on computer systems, software development, programming, algorithms, networking, databases, artificial intelligence, and much more. It’s one of the most in-demand degrees today because the world is running on tech — from mobile apps and websites to smart cars and AI chatbots. In the first year, students usually cover core engineering subjects like Maths, Physics, Chemistry, and Basics of Programming (usually in C or Python). From the second year onwards, the focus shifts heavily toward computer-related subjects such as Data Structures, Object-Oriented Programming, Operating Systems, Computer Networks, Database Management Systems (DBMS), Web Development, Software Engineering, and Artificial Intelligence. Alongside theoretical knowledge, students also do lab sessions, coding assignments, mini projects, and internships that prepare them for real-world problem-solving. Over the 4 years, students are expected to gain strong skills in coding (Python, Java, C++), development tools (Git, VS Code), frameworks (like React, Django), and cloud platforms (AWS, Azure, etc.). In the final year, a major capstone project allows students to apply everything they’ve learned in building a full software or system solution.
